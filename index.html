About Me

I am a fifth year graduate student at the UVa Department of Astronomy. My research focuses on star formation in (Ultra-)Luminous Infrared Galaxies in the local Universe, and I am part of the multi-wavelength research collaboration GOALS. I work closely with radio data taken by the Very Large Array located in New Mexico, with supports from a group of awesome local collaborators and my dissertation advisor Dr. Aaron Evans at UVa/NRAO. I am also a member of the assessment team for Dark Skies, Bright Kids!, and a current grad repsentative of our department's Diversity, Inclusion & Equity committee. In my down time, I especially enjoy playing music, hiking and learning languages.
